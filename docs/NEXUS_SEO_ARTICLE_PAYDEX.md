# What Is a PAYDEX Score and How to Build One Fast (2026 Guide)

If you've ever applied for business credit and been denied — or received less than you asked for — there's a good chance your PAYDEX score played a role.

Most business owners focus on their personal credit score. But lenders and suppliers who extend business credit are often looking at something entirely different: your business credit profile, and specifically, your PAYDEX score.

In this guide, you'll learn exactly what a PAYDEX score is, why it matters, and how to build one from scratch — even if your business is brand new.

---

## What Is a PAYDEX Score?

A PAYDEX score is a business credit score issued by **Dun & Bradstreet (D&B)**, one of the three major business credit bureaus. It specifically measures how promptly your business pays its bills and financial obligations.

PAYDEX scores range from **1 to 100**:

| Score Range | Payment Behavior | What It Signals |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 80–100 | Paid early or on time | Low risk — preferred by lenders |
| 70–79 | Paid up to 15 days late | Moderate risk |
| 60–69 | Paid 16–30 days late | Some risk |
| 50–59 | Paid 31–60 days late | Higher risk |
| Below 50 | Paid 61+ days late | High risk — affects approvals |

**Key insight:** A PAYDEX score of 80 means you pay exactly on time. To score **above 80**, you need to pay **early** — before the due date.

---

## Why Does the PAYDEX Score Matter?

Your PAYDEX score affects:

**1. Business credit approvals**  
Suppliers, vendors, and lenders check your PAYDEX score when deciding whether to extend net-30, net-60, or net-90 payment terms. A strong score means more flexibility and better terms.

**2. Business credit card approvals**  
Many business credit card issuers pull your business credit profile — not just your personal score. A strong PAYDEX score makes approval more likely.

**3. SBA loan and bank loan applications**  
Traditional lenders often review your full business credit profile, which includes your D&B file. A blank or low PAYDEX score is a red flag.

**4. Vendor relationships**  
Suppliers offering trade credit — where you pay after receiving goods — use PAYDEX to assess risk. A high score unlocks better payment terms.

**5. Insurance premiums**  
Some commercial insurers factor in business credit when pricing premiums.

## What's the Difference Between PAYDEX and Your Personal Credit Score?

| Factor | Personal Credit (FICO) | PAYDEX Score |
|--------|----------------------|--------------|
| Issued by | Equifax, Experian, TransUnion | Dun & Bradstreet |
| Range | 300–850 | 1–100 |
| Based on | Personal payment history | Business payment history only |
| Affects | Personal loans, cards, mortgages | Business credit, vendor terms |
| Good score | 700+ | 75+ (80 is the target) |
| Linked to you | Yes (SSN) | Your business EIN |

Critical point: **Your personal credit and business credit are separate.** Building your PAYDEX score does not require a personal credit check and does not affect your personal credit score.

## What's the Difference Between PAYDEX and Other Business Credit Scores?

The three major business credit bureaus each have their own scoring systems:

| Bureau | Score Name | Range | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Dun & Bradstreet | PAYDEX | 1–100 | Payment promptness |
| Experian Business | Intelliscore Plus | 1–100 | Overall credit risk |
| Equifax Business | Business Credit Risk Score | 101–992 | Risk of late payment |

Most lenders check at least two of these. Your goal is to build a positive profile on all three.

---

## How Is a PAYDEX Score Calculated?

Dun & Bradstreet calculates your PAYDEX score based on **payment experiences** reported by your vendors and creditors.

To have a PAYDEX score at all, you need:
- At least **3 trade references** reporting to D&B
- At least **2 experiences in the last 90 days**

Without these, your D&B file will show "not enough data" — which some lenders treat as a red flag.

The score is **dollar-weighted**: larger payments carry more influence. If you pay a $1,000 invoice early but a $100 invoice late, the early payment has more weight.

## How to Build a PAYDEX Score from Scratch

If your business is new or has no D&B credit history, here's the exact process:

### Step 1: Get Your DUNS Number (Free)

A DUNS (Data Universal Numbering System) number is your business's unique identifier with Dun & Bradstreet. You need one to have a PAYDEX score.

1. Go to **dnb.com**
2. Search for your business
3. If not found, register for a free DUNS number
4. Allow 30 days for processing (expedited options available)

### Step 2: Ensure Your Business Information Is Accurate

Before building credit, make sure D&B has accurate information:
- Legal business name (exactly as registered)
- Business address (not a PO box)
- Phone number
- Industry/SIC code
- Year established
- Number of employees

### Step 3: Open Net-30 Vendor Accounts

Net-30 accounts are supplier accounts where you purchase goods/services and pay the invoice within 30 days. When these suppliers report your payments to D&B, it builds your PAYDEX score.

**Starter vendors (approve new businesses, report to D&B):**

| Vendor | What They Sell | Approval Ease | Reports To |
|--------|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Uline | Packaging, shipping supplies | Easy | D&B, Experian |
| Quill | Office supplies | Easy | D&B, Equifax |
| Grainger | Tools, maintenance supplies | Moderate | D&B |
| Crown Office Supplies | Office supplies | Easy | D&B |
| Summa Office Supplies | Office supplies | Easy | D&B |
| Home Depot Business | Hardware, building supplies | Moderate | D&B, Experian |

**How to use these accounts:**
1. Apply using your business EIN (not SSN)
2. Purchase small items you actually need ($25–$100 initially)
3. **Pay the invoice before the due date** (pay on day 20 of net-30, not day 30)
4. Repeat monthly

### Step 4: Pay Early, Not Just On Time

This is the most common mistake: paying on the due date gets you a PAYDEX of 80. Paying **before** the due date pushes you toward 90–100.

Set payment reminders for day 20 of each net-30 invoice.

### Step 5: Monitor Your D&B Profile

Check your D&B profile at least monthly to:
- Verify trade lines are being reported
- Dispute any inaccurate information
- Track your PAYDEX score improvement

## Common PAYDEX Score Mistakes

**1. Paying on the due date instead of early**  
On-time = PAYDEX 80. Pay 10–15 days early = PAYDEX 90+.

**2. Too few trade lines**  
Three is the minimum to generate a score. Aim for 5–7 to build a robust profile.

**3. Using vendors that don't report to D&B**  
Not all vendors report to credit bureaus. Confirm before opening accounts.

**4. Mixing personal and business finances**  
Use your business EIN, not your SSN, for all business accounts. Personal and business credit are separate systems.

**5. Ignoring the other bureaus**  
D&B's PAYDEX matters, but Experian Business and Equifax Business do too. Open accounts that report to all three.

**6. Not monitoring for errors**  
Inaccurate information can drag down your score. Check your D&B file regularly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a DUNS number free?**  
Yes. You can register for a DUNS number at dnb.com at no cost.

**Does applying for a DUNS number affect my personal credit?**  
No. A DUNS number is for your business and does not trigger a personal credit inquiry.

**How many trade lines do I need to build a strong PAYDEX score?**  
Minimum 3 to generate a score; 5–7 to build a robust profile.

**Can I have a PAYDEX score if my business is a sole proprietorship?**  
Yes, but a limited liability company (LLC) or corporation creates a cleaner separation between personal and business credit.

**What happens if I pay late?**  
Late payments lower your PAYDEX score. Consistent late payment can drop your score below 60, which signals high risk to lenders. Pay early whenever possible.
