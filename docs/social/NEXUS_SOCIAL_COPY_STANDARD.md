# Nexus Social Copy Standard

Nexus copy must sell a practical transformation without making illegal or unrealistic promises. The voice is confident, direct, opportunity-focused, and useful for entrepreneurs, small business owners, credit/funding seekers, side hustlers, and business builders.

## Required Structure

Every social post should include:

1. Hook: stop the scroll with a specific business or funding pain.
2. Pain/problem: name the real-world friction.
3. Insight: teach one useful idea.
4. Nexus solution: connect the idea to the Credit/Funding + Opportunity operating system.
5. Offer/CTA: ask for READY, checklist, newsletter, or the $97 Starter Review.
6. Compliance-safe wording: readiness, preparation, possible gaps, no guarantees.

## Platform Rules

Facebook posts can be longer, more conversational, and more story/problem oriented. Use one clear CTA.

Instagram captions should be tighter, visual, and built around reels/carousels. Use line breaks and concise hooks.

Short videos need a 3-second hook, one problem, one practical insight, the Nexus angle, and a single CTA.

Carousel concepts should have 5-7 slides with one idea per slide and a final CTA.

Landing pages must include hero promise, problem, audience, what Nexus reviews, the $97 starter offer, subscription path, objections/FAQ, disclaimer, and CTA.

Newsletters should teach one topic, give a checklist-style action, and point to the $97 review or monthly support.

## Banned Claims

Do not claim guaranteed funding, guaranteed approval, guaranteed deletions, guaranteed score increase, guaranteed credit repair, guaranteed credit card approvals, guaranteed dollar amounts, or that funding will not show on personal credit unless properly qualified and verified.

## Allowed Language

Use funding readiness, credit profile review, possible inaccuracies, unverifiable/outdated items, dispute workflow support, business bankability preparation, Tier 1 funding preparation, 0% interest business credit opportunities when qualified, may qualify, prepare before applying, identify what may be holding you back, and build a funding blueprint.
