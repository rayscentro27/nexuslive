# X Posts — funding readiness strategy

Here are 5 standalone X posts on "funding readiness strategy" that deliver value without hashtags:  

---  

**POST 1:**  
Most founders think funding is about traction. Wrong. It’s about predictability. Investors bet on *repeatable* revenue, not spikes. If your growth looks random, you’re not ready. Fix the model first.  

**POST 2:**  
"Clean cap table" doesn’t just mean few shareholders. It means no skeletons: weird vesting terms, side deals, or unresolved debt. Surprise an investor here, and the conversation ends fast. Audit early.  

**POST 3:**  
Your pitch deck isn’t a brochure. It’s a tension builder. Investors scroll fast—hook them in 3 slides: the problem (ugly), your solution (elegant), and why now (urgent). Save the rest for diligence.  

**POST 4:**  
Revenue ≠ fundability. A $1M business with 90% margins and no scalability gets passed over. A $200K biz with clear 10X potential gets checks. Position for the future, not the present.  

**POST 5:**  
The best time to build investor relationships? 6-12 months *before* you need cash. By the time you ask, they should already know your name, team, and vision. Cold outreach = desperation tax.
