**POST 1:**  
Most founders think funding readiness = pitch deck + financials. Wrong. It’s about proving demand. No traction? No check. Build first, fund later.  

**POST 2:**  
Investors don’t fund ideas. They fund execution. Your "funding readiness" starts with revenue—even if it’s small. No revenue? Get creative. Pre-sell.  

**POST 3:**  
A polished pitch deck won’t save a weak business model. Funding readiness means answering: "Why will this scale?" before an investor asks.  

**POST 4:**  
The best funding strategy? Don’t need it. Bootstrap until investors chase YOU. Leverage forces them to say yes—on your terms.  

**POST 5:**  
"Funding readiness" is a myth. The best founders raise when they don’t *need* it. Control the narrative, or the market will control you.
