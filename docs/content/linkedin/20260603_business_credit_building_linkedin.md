# LinkedIn Post — business credit building

**Business credit isn't just about borrowing – it's about unlocking leverage most founders never tap into.**

Let's be real: relying *only* on personal credit for your business is like walking a tightrope without a net. It limits your funding options, puts your personal assets at constant risk, and caps your growth potential before you even start scaling. You're essentially handcuffing your company's future.

The problem? Most owners get stuck in the "personal guarantee loop" or think business credit is too complex or only for massive corporations. They miss the strategic advantage.

**Building strong business credit is a deliberate process, not luck. Here’s the core framework:**

1.  **Establish the Foundation:** Get your business legally solid (LLC/Corp), secure an EIN, get a dedicated business phone/address, and register for a DUNS number. This separates *you* from the *business* in the credit world's eyes.
2.  **Activate Starter Tradelines:** Open accounts with *starter vendors* (Net-30 terms) who report payments to business credit bureaus (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business, Equifax Business). Think office supplies, specific SaaS tools, or business fuel cards. **Use them and pay EARLY or ON TIME.**
3.  **Scale Strategically:** Once initial tradelines report positively, leverage that history to access higher-limit business credit cards, store cards, and eventually lines of credit – *without* personal guarantees. This is where real financial firepower kicks in.

This separation builds credibility, protects your personal finances, and unlocks capital on dramatically better terms. It’s foundational financial hygiene.
