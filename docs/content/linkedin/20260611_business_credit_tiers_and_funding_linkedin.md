# LinkedIn Post — business credit tiers and funding

**Here’s your LinkedIn authority post on business credit tiers and funding:**

**Most founders think business credit is just a bigger personal score – and that misconception kills funding opportunities.**

You hustle, hit revenue targets, yet that crucial loan or line of credit gets denied. Why? Banks and lenders see your business through a lens *you* might not understand: credit tiers.

They categorize businesses (often silently) into tiers based on credit strength. Stuck in "Starter" or "Personal Guarantee" tiers? You face higher rates, lower limits, or flat-out rejections, even if cash flow looks good. Relying solely on personal credit caps your growth and piles on risk.

**Here’s the shift: Building *intentional* business credit unlocks higher tiers and better capital:**

1.  **Tiers Are Real:** Business credit isn't one score. Lenders group you (e.g., Starter, Marketplace, Corporate) based on your biz credit reports (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian, Equifax), tradelines, and financials. Higher tiers mean better terms.
2.  **Separation is Power:** Your SSN isn't the key; your EIN is. Establishing credit purely under your business name (with vendors, net-30 accounts, cards) builds a profile *independent* of your personal score. This is non-negotiable.
3.  **Tier-Jumping is Strategic:** It's not luck. It requires establishing foundational tradelines, then strategically adding higher-tier accounts that report to the *right* bureaus. This signals reliability to lenders.

Stop letting hidden tiers limit your funding. Building strong business credit is a core growth strategy, not an afterthought.
