**Your Payment History is Costing You More Than You Think**  
Cash flow is tight for every business right now. Yet most owners overlook a critical lever controlling their access to capital and supplier terms: their PAYDEX score. A sub-80 score means higher interest rates, rejected loans, and strained vendor relationships. Fixing this isn't about gaming the system – it’s about strategic payment behavior that unlocks liquidity.  

**THE PROBLEM: "On Time" Isn't Good Enough**  
Most businesses believe paying invoices *by* the due date protects their credit. They're wrong. Dun & Bradstreet's PAYDEX (scored 1-100) heavily penalizes even slightly late payments but offers its biggest rewards for **early** payments. Worse, many suppliers don’t report to D&B at all, meaning your punctuality goes unnoticed. Relying solely on net-30/60 terms without strategic reporting leaves your score – and borrowing power – stagnant.  

**THE INTELLIGENCE: Leverage the 15-Day Window**  
PAYDEX isn't just about avoiding negatives; it’s about aggressively accumulating positives. D&B weights payments made **15+ days early** most favorably. The key insight? **Not all payments are equal.** Focus on:  
1.  **Supplier Selection:** Prioritize vendors who *actually report* to D&B (confirm this!). Paying non-reporters does nothing for your score.  
2.  **Term Conversion:** Shift key suppliers from net terms (e.g., Net 30) to revolving credit accounts *that report*. Revolving lines show consistent, responsible usage.  
3.  **The Early Pay Bump:** A payment 15 days early counts significantly more than one paid exactly on time.  

**THE ACTION: Optimize Your PAYDEX in 72 Hours**  
1.  **Audit & Activate:** Log into your free D&B CreditBuilder profile *today*. Verify all listed trade lines are accurate. Dispute errors immediately. Identify which high-value suppliers report – focus payments here first.  
2.  **Target 2-3 Key Invoices:** Identify 2-3 invoices from *reporting* suppliers due in the next 30 days. Pay them **10-15 days early** this week. This creates instant positive data points.  
3.  **Negotiate Reporting Terms:** Call one major supplier. Ask: "Do you report payments to Dun & Bradstreet?" If no, propose: "If I switch this account to a revolving line and pay early, will you agree to report?" Get it in writing.
