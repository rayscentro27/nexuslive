## SCRIPT 1 — "Why Your Business Gets Rejected for Funding (And How to Fix It)"

**Target keyword:** why businesses get denied business loans  
**Search intent:** Problem-aware, seeking diagnosis  
**Estimated CPC:** $4–8 | **CPM potential:** $10–15

---

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**

"If you've ever applied for a business loan or line of credit and got denied — or got a lower amount than you asked for — this video is going to show you exactly why that happened. And more importantly, exactly what to do about it."

"By the end of this, you'll understand what lenders are actually looking at — and it's probably not what you think."

---

**[INTRO — 0:30–1:30]**

"Most business owners think getting denied means their credit score was too low. And yes, credit matters. But it's not the whole picture."

"Lenders use what's called a fundability framework — they're looking at multiple signals across your business profile. And most business owners have blind spots in 3 to 5 of these areas."

"Today I'm breaking down the 8 areas lenders check, why most businesses fail at least 3 of them, and what you can do right now to improve your position."

---

**[BODY — 1:30–9:00]**

**Area 1: Business Credit Profile (2:00)**

"Most business owners don't know that your business has its own credit score — completely separate from your personal credit. It's called your PAYDEX score on Dun & Bradstreet, and your Intelliscore on Experian Business."

"If you've never applied for credit in your business name, your business credit profile is essentially blank. Lenders hate blank. Blank means risk."

"The fix: Start building trade lines now. Net-30 accounts with vendors like Uline, Quill, or Grainger — accounts that report your payment history to business credit bureaus."

**Area 2: Time in Business (3:00)**

"Many lenders have a minimum time in business requirement — typically 1–2 years. If you're under that threshold, you're going to get denied regardless of everything else."

"Newer businesses: focus on lenders who serve startups — CDFI lenders, microloans, and revenue-based financing options."

**Area 3: Revenue Documentation (4:00)**

"Can you prove your revenue? Business bank statements, tax returns, accounting records — lenders want 3–6 months of bank statements minimum."

"If your revenue is inconsistent, or you're running personal and business expenses through the same account, that's a red flag."

**Area 4: Banking Relationship (5:00)**

"Your bank matters. Established business checking account, average daily balance, no NSFs — these are signals."

"The fix: Open a dedicated business checking account if you haven't. Keep a minimum balance. Let it season for 6+ months."

**Area 5: Business Structure and Documentation (6:00)**

"Is your business registered properly? EIN, state registration, business license, operating agreement — lenders verify these."

"If your business address is a PO box or your home address, some lenders will flag that. Consider a virtual business address."

**Area 6–8: (Industry Risk, Existing Debt, Tax Compliance) (7:00)**

"High-risk industries — cannabis, firearms, adult entertainment — have fewer lending options. Know your industry risk profile."

"Existing debt load: lenders calculate your Debt Service Coverage Ratio. Too much existing debt relative to revenue = denied."

"Tax compliance: if you have outstanding tax liens, IRS issues, or unpaid payroll taxes — clear those first."

## SCRIPT 2 — "Build Business Credit from $0 in 90 Days (Step by Step)"

**Target keyword:** how to build business credit fast  
**Search intent:** Solution-seeking, high-intent  
**Estimated CPM:** $12–18

---

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]**

"In this video I'm giving you the exact 90-day roadmap to build business credit from nothing — even if your business is brand new, even if you have bad personal credit."

"This is the same process that credit consultants charge $2,000 to walk you through. I'm giving it to you in 10 minutes."

---

**[BODY — Key Beats]**

**Days 1–30: Foundation**
- Form the business correctly (LLC or Corp, not sole prop)
- Get EIN from IRS.gov (free, 5 minutes)
- Open dedicated business checking account
- Get a business phone number (VoIP counts)
- Register with Dun & Bradstreet (get DUNS number — free)
- Register with Experian Business and Equifax Business

**Days 31–60: First Trade Lines**
- Apply for "starter" vendor accounts that don't check personal credit:
  - Uline (office/shipping supplies)
  - Quill (office supplies)
  - Grainger (tools/maintenance) — approval: 30 days
  - Crown Office Supplies
  - Summa Office Supplies
- Buy something small from each, pay immediately
- These report to DNB and Experian

**Days 61–90: Tier 2 Accounts**
- Apply for fleet cards (WEX, Fuel Man) — report to business bureaus
- Apply for store business cards (Home Depot Business, Staples Business)
- Apply for first business credit card (Secured business card if needed)

**PAYDEX score target: 75+ after 90 days with consistent on-time payments**
