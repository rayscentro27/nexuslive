Credit repair is the process of fixing your credit score by disputing inaccurate items on your credit report. Your FICO score is calculated using five factors: payment history at 35%, credit utilization at 30%, length of credit history at 15%, credit mix at 10%, and new credit at 10%.

One of the most effective strategies is sending dispute letters to the credit bureaus Equifax, TransUnion, and Experian via certified mail. Online disputes are often dismissed more quickly, while written disputes require a 30-day investigation window under the FCRA.

Becoming an authorized user on a credit card account with low utilization and a long history can add significant positive tradeline history to your credit report within 30 to 60 days. This is known as tradeline piggybacking.

Credit utilization below 10 percent on each individual card and below 10 percent overall is considered optimal for maximizing your FICO score. Paying down revolving balances before the statement closes helps lower the reported utilization.

Filing a CFPB complaint after a failed bureau dispute often results in a second investigation and increases the chance of removal. Always keep copies of all correspondence sent.

Risk warning: Paying a collection agency may restart the statute of limitations for debt collection lawsuits in some states. Check your state laws before making any payment on an old debt.

Opportunity: Offering credit repair consulting services as a business is viable under the Credit Repair Organizations Act as long as you do not charge upfront fees before services are rendered.
