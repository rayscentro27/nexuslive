# Nexus Social Media Compliance Guardrails
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-11  
**Applies to:** All social media content on all platforms  
**Owner:** Raymond Davis / GoClearOnline

---

## Purpose

This document defines the compliance rules for all Nexus social media content. These rules protect the brand, protect viewers, and ensure content meets FTC, SEC, and platform policy requirements.

All content creators and AI-assisted drafting must follow these rules before any piece is approved for publishing.

---

## Rule 1 — No Guaranteed Funding Claims

**Prohibited:**
- "Get approved for funding"
- "This will get you a loan"
- "Guaranteed funding for your business"
- "We'll get you funded"
- "Apply and get approved"

**Required framing:**
- "Improve your business's fundability"
- "Learn how to qualify for funding"
- "See if your business is funding-ready"
- "Steps to position your business for capital access"

**Why:** Business funding approval depends on lender decisions, creditworthiness, and factors outside Nexus's control. Implying guarantees violates FTC truthfulness standards and could expose the brand to liability.

---

## Rule 2 — No Guaranteed Income Claims

**Prohibited:**
- "Make $X per month"
- "I made $X using this system"
- "This method generates passive income"
- "Turn your side hustle into a $10k/month business guaranteed"

**Required framing:**
- "Results vary based on individual effort and circumstances"
- "Income depends on many factors including your business, market, and execution"
- "This is educational — your results may differ"

**Why:** Implied income guarantees violate FTC guidelines on income claims. They also attract the wrong audience and create customer expectation misalignment.

---

## Rule 3 — No Investment Advice

**Prohibited:**
- Recommending specific stocks, ETFs, or assets to buy or sell
- Telling viewers when to enter or exit trades
- Saying "invest in X" or "avoid X"
- Implying Nexus is a registered investment advisor

**Required framing:**
- "This is educational content about [market concept]"
- "Always consult a licensed financial advisor before making investment decisions"
- "This is not investment advice"

**Why:** Providing investment advice without SEC registration as an RIA or broker-dealer is illegal. Nexus is not a licensed financial advisor.

---

## Rule 4 — Educational Only Disclaimer

Every video, post, and piece of content must be framed as educational:

**Minimum disclaimer (short-form):**
> "Educational only. Results vary. Not financial or legal advice."

**Full disclaimer (long-form videos and descriptions):**
> "This content is for educational and informational purposes only. Nothing in this video/post constitutes financial, legal, investment, credit, or tax advice. Individual results vary. No specific outcomes are guaranteed. Consult a licensed professional before making financial decisions."

**Where to include:**
- YouTube video descriptions (always)
- YouTube verbal mention (recommended for any results discussion)
- TikTok/Reels text overlay (when discussing income, credit, or funding results)
- LinkedIn posts discussing strategies with outcome implications

---

## Rule 5 — Trading Content: Paper/Demo Only

**Prohibited:**
- Sharing live trade entries or exits
- Claiming to trade live capital
- Providing trade signals
- Showing real P&L as proof of strategy

**Required:**
- Label all trading content as "Paper/Demo Trading"
- Include overlay or verbal: "Paper trading only — not live funds"
- Prominent disclaimer on any performance-related content
- Never compare paper trading results to potential live trading income

**Why:** Publishing live trade signals may require FINRA registration. Even educational trading content must be clearly labeled to avoid implying professional advisory services.

---

## Rule 6 — "Results Vary" Disclosure

Any content that shows or implies results (credit scores, funding amounts, business growth) must include:

> "Results vary. Individual outcomes depend on your specific business circumstances, creditworthiness, and effort."

**Where this is required:**
- Any case study or "success story" content
- Before/after demonstrations
- Any content showing credit score changes
- Any content showing funding amounts received

---

## Rule 7 — Avoid "Get Rich Quick" Framing

**Prohibited framing:**
- "Quit your job with this one trick"
- "The secret banks don't want you to know"
- "How I made $X in 30 days"
- "Easy money" or "fast money"

**Allowed framing:**
- "The step-by-step process takes time, but here's how to do it right"
- "This isn't overnight — it takes 60–90 days to build business credit properly"
- "Here's the honest timeline most people don't talk about"

**Why:** Get-rich-quick framing attracts poor-fit customers, invites regulatory scrutiny, and damages long-term brand trust.

---

## Rule 8 — Platform-Specific Policies

| Platform | Key Policy |
|----------|-----------|
| YouTube | No misleading thumbnails or titles; follow YouTube monetization policies for financial content |
| Instagram | No misleading business claims; sponsored content must be disclosed |
| TikTok | Financial content must be accurate; no misleading earnings claims |
| LinkedIn | Professional tone; no spammy "financial freedom" language |
| Facebook | Financial services content may require additional review under Meta's ad policies |
| X/Twitter | No coordinated inauthentic behavior; no misleading claims |

---

## Content Review Checklist

Before approving any content for publishing, verify:

- [ ] No guaranteed funding or income claims
- [ ] No investment advice
- [ ] Educational disclaimer present (appropriate level for content)
- [ ] Trading content labeled paper/demo
- [ ] "Results vary" disclosure on any outcome-implying content
- [ ] No get-rich-quick framing
- [ ] Accurate factual claims (verify stats and numbers before publishing)
- [ ] No misleading headlines or thumbnails
- [ ] CTA does not overstate what Nexus can deliver
- [ ] Sponsored/paid partnerships disclosed (if applicable)
