## Script 1: How to Make Your Business Fundable Before Applying for Credit

### Hook
If you apply for business credit before fixing these five fundamentals, you are increasing your odds of denial.

### Intro
In this video, I will walk you through a simple fundability framework you can use before submitting any application. This is for education and planning, not financial or legal advice.

### Educational Content
Step 1: Business identity consistency.
Your legal name, website, email domain, phone listing, and public profiles should match. Lenders flag mismatches quickly.

Step 2: Entity and compliance hygiene.
Make sure your LLC or corporation is active, your filings are current, and your operating records are organized.

Step 3: Business banking credibility.
Use a dedicated business account. Keep clean transaction patterns and avoid mixing personal and business activity.

Step 4: Credit-readiness basics.
Review your business profile data, verify reporting details, and fix errors before applying.

Step 5: Application sequencing.
Do not apply everywhere at once. Start with the most realistic path, then expand based on outcomes.

## Script 2: 5 Business Funding Mistakes That Get You Denied

### Hook
Most funding denials are caused by preventable mistakes, not bad luck.

### Intro
Today we are covering five common mistakes that block business funding approvals and what to do instead.

### Educational Content
Mistake 1: Applying before profile cleanup.
Fix identity inconsistencies and missing records first.

Mistake 2: Weak business documentation.
Have formation docs, compliance records, and business details organized.

Mistake 3: No funding strategy.
Random applications create unnecessary risk and confusion.

Mistake 4: Ignoring readiness signals.
If your setup has major gaps, address them before any hard push.

Mistake 5: No post-denial recovery plan.
Denial should trigger a structured correction plan, not panic applications.

Use trusted databases and verified organizations instead of random social posts.

Part 2: Fit filter.
Check eligibility first: industry, location, stage, and required documents.

Track close dates, submission requirements, and follow-up status in one place.

Part 4: Reusable application assets.
Maintain a core package: mission summary, impact statement, and baseline business profile.

Part 5: Opportunity pipeline.
Treat grants like a pipeline: discovered, qualified, in-progress, submitted, closed.
